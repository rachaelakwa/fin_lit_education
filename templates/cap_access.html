What is Capital Access?

As the name implies capital access describes an individuals ability to access capital. This ability is controlled by credit.
Credit comes from the Latin word 'Credo' which means 'I believe'. Thus the popular term 'credit score' is an individual's belief score, and for countries, this is a 'credit rating'.

How are they related?

Access to capital debt is multi-dimensional:

  • How much can be borrowed?
  • When does the capital have to be repaid?
  • How much will it cost to borrow?
  • What are the penalties for missing payments?

Overview of Capital and Financing Methods

Equity Capital Equity Capital, also known as risk capital, is the personal investment of the owners in a company, making them partial owners. It doesn't require repayment but carries the risk of loss if the business fails. Sources include personal savings, friends and family, angel investors, and venture capitalists.

Debt Capital Debt Capital involves funds borrowed by an entrepreneur that must be repaid with interest, typically through loans from banks or financial institutions. It allows entrepreneurs to retain ownership but requires regular debt service payments, which can be challenging for new businesses.

Initial Public Offering (IPO) An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is when a private company sells shares to the public for the first time to raise equity capital. This can lead to significant growth but also requires compliance with regulatory standards and exposes the company to market and public scrutiny.

Crowdfunding Crowdfunding leverages social networking to raise capital, allowing entrepreneurs to post their pitches on specialized websites and attract investments from ordinary people. It democratizes the investment process but requires an effective pitch and marketing strategy.

Bootstrapping Bootstrapping involves using personal savings and low-cost methods to start a business, avoiding external financing and maintaining full ownership. It fosters resourcefulness and efficiency but may limit growth due to funding constraints. Successful bootstrapped businesses may attract investors later.
